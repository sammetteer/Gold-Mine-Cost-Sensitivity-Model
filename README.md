# Brucejack Gold Mine – Cost & Valuation Model (2026)

A full mining‑economics model of the Brucejack gold mine, built to demonstrate integrated technical–financial analysis. This project combines geological understanding with valuation, cost modeling, and sensitivity analysis.

---

# Executive Summary

Brucejack is a high‑grade underground gold mine operating at 1.387 Mtpa with a head grade of 7.5 g/t, producing ~330–340 koz Au annually. Under a 2026 base case gold price of **$4,500/oz**, the model returns a pre‑tax **NPV of \$8.23B** (5% discount rate assumed).

A fully loaded **AISC of \$1,108/oz** places Brucejack in a competitive cost position, with strong valuation leverage to gold price and grade. Sensitivity analysis shows gold price and grade as the dominant drivers of project value, followed by sustaining capital and operating costs.

---

# Key Assumptions

- **Gold price:** $4,500/oz (sensitized $3,500–$5,500/oz)  
- **Mine life:** 10 years  
- **Throughput:** 1,387,000 tonnes per year  
- **Head grade:** 7.5 g/t (sensitized 0.8×–1.2×)  
- **Recovery:** ~95–96%  
- **Discount rate:** 5%  
- **AISC:** $1,108/oz (LOM, fully loaded)  
- **Cost components:** mining, processing, G&A, sustaining capital, royalties  

---

# Model Outputs

- **Base case NPV:** \$8,231,587,406  
- **AISC:** \$1,108/oz  
- **Annual gold production:** ~330–340 koz Au  
- **Mine life:** 10 years  
- **Strongest sensitivities:** gold price, grade
